[Sub1L PC System Identification]

This is a simple guide to pool my knowledge of mini's into a quick glance guide, in the case you come across a system you want to immediately identify, and you can't immediately find specifications for.
These naming schemes are simple once you get used to them, and are even a bit interchangeable.
Note: Both HP and Lenovo denote AMD by using an ending 5 in the 2-3 digit ENTERPRISE name.

[HP PRODESK/ELITEDESK MINI]

Build Quality
4xx"ProDesk" - Tool-less Entry, with basic functionality, nearing home use.
6xx"ProDesk" - Tool-less Assembly, with basic Enterprise support features.
7xx"EliteDesk" - Effectively the same as 8xx, but seems to be AMD specific.
8xx"EliteDesk" - Military Standard, and full featured for Enterprise use.

[DELL OPTIPLEX MICRO]

Build Quality
3xxxEntry-Level, usually built for light/general use.
5xxxMid-range
7xxxHigh-End, usually designed with a specific function in mind.
9xxxRefer to 7xxxx
Other Signifiers
x0xxAlways 0. The number denotes a screensize, based on 2X inches for desktop and 1X inches for laptops, X being this number.
xxx0Always 0. This is for laptop naming, as a variant for convertable versions.
OptiPlex Chipset

xx204xxxT - Haswell
xx406xxxT - Sky lake

xx9011xxxT - Rocket Lake (Theoretical)
xx0012xxxT - Alder Lake
